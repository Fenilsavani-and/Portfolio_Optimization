## üß† Executive Summary
This project presents a **hybrid portfolio optimization framework** that integrates **traditional financial theories** with **advanced Deep Neural Network (DNN) architectures** to improve risk-adjusted returns under real-world market constraints.  
The research is **peer-reviewed and published at an IEEE International Conference**, validating both its academic rigor and practical applicability.

The model combines **Markowitz-based statistical optimization**, **factor-driven models**, and **deep learning techniques** (LSTM, TCN, Reinforcement Learning-inspired optimization) to dynamically allocate asset weights across varying market regimes such as **bullish, bearish, and inflation-driven environments**.

---

## üíº Business Problem
Traditional portfolio optimization techniques (e.g., Mean-Variance Optimization) suffer from:

- High sensitivity to input assumptions  
- Poor performance under non-linear and volatile market conditions  
- Limited adaptability to regime shifts  
- Inefficiency in high-dimensional asset universes  

From a **business and investment perspective**, fund managers require:
- Stable portfolio construction  
- Robust downside risk control  
- Improved Sharpe ratios under real constraints  
- Scalable and explainable optimization frameworks  

This project addresses these challenges by **bridging financial theory with machine learning** to enhance portfolio performance and decision-making.
